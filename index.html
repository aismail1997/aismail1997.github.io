About us

We are a group of researchers and engineers passionate about community development, technology, and healthcare. We are working on developing technology that can help communities and health providers make data-driven health decisions.

Data Collection

We build upon existing open source data collection tools such as DHIS2 and Local Ground to allow for customized data collection. The collected data is fed into our data analysis pipeline.

Disease Prediction and Tracking

Our disease prediction algorithms help identify communities that are at risk of disease and the underlying factors that may be responsible for the threat.

Visualization

We present user-friendly insights to health providers such as doctors and front-line health workers to help them make data-driven decisions.

Our Vision

We believe that the future of healthcare lies in communities making their own decisions and taking action to improve their health. With an emphasis on patient autonomy and human-centered design, we hope to make healthcare accessible to all, globally. Chitra, which means "representation" in Sanskrit, is our effort to help build and make available a picture of a community's health to the community and health providers.

Projected Timeline

Design Iteration and Scale-up

April - July 2018
Based on our findings, we will fine tune our models for disease. We will also carry out design exercises with users following an iterative human-centered process. With our updated software, we will carry out a second deployment (location undecided).

Initial Pilot

February - March 2018
Our initial pilot will be in Delhi, India. Our planned deployment will be with 10 frontline-health workers in peri-urban areas of Delhi, covering a population of approximately 1000 people from a range of socioeconomic strata. To measure effectiveness, we will compare results against a control group of 10
front-line health workers who will be provided only the data collection application.

Prototype Development

January 2018
We are currently in the prototyping phase. This includes the development of an Android application for data collection and visualization and models for disease prediction.
